Exoplanet & Brown Dwarf Polarimetry

University of California, Santa Barbara & Caltech

Projects at UCSB & Caltech

Brown Dwarf Disks (Caltech): This project focused on optimizing the data reduction pipeline for the WIRC+Pol instrument and using near-infrared spectropolarimetry to characterize the circumstellar disks around brown dwarfs in the Taurus Molecular Cloud. This helps us understand the early stages of planet formation around these low-mass objects.

Exoplanet Polarimetry (UCSB): This research involved attempting to detect polarized light from the unresolved Luhman 16 brown dwarf binary system. A successful detection would provide valuable information about the atmospheric properties, such as cloud bands and rotational flattening, of these nearby objects.
